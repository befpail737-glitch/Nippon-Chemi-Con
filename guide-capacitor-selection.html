Step-by-Step Selection Process

Step 1: Define Application Requirements

+

Start by determining your specific application requirements:

  • Operating voltage: Determine maximum continuous voltage and any transient conditions
  • Required capacitance: Calculate necessary capacitance for filtering, energy storage, or timing
  • Ripple current: Calculate expected RMS ripple current under all operating conditions
  • Temperature range: Define ambient operating temperature and any internal heating effects
  • Physical constraints: Board space, height limitations, mounting requirements
  • Life expectancy: Required operational life in hours under specific conditions

Step 2: Voltage Selection

+

Select a capacitor with a rated voltage at least 20-50% higher than the maximum applied voltage:

  • For 400V application: Use 500V or 550V rated capacitor
  • For 200V application: Use 250V or 350V rated capacitor
  • Higher derating improves reliability and life expectancy
  • Consider transient voltage conditions and voltage doubling in certain topologies

Step 3: Ripple Current Evaluation

+

Calculate the actual ripple current in your application:

Compare with NCC's rated ripple current values at your operating frequency and temperature. Consider:

  • Harmonic content of ripple current
  • Effect of parallel capacitors on current sharing
  • Derating for ambient temperature conditions
  • Thermal effects from surrounding components

If ripple current exceeds 50% of rated value, consider enhanced ripple series (KY) or parallel configuration.

Step 5: Package and Mounting Selection

+

Choose the appropriate mounting method based on your application:

  • Radial Lead: General purpose, prototyping, repair-friendly
  • SMD: High-volume automated assembly, space-constrained applications
  • Snap-in: High capacitance, easy mechanical retention
  • Screw Terminal: High current, high capacitance, permanent installations

Application-Specific Recommendations

Power Supply Applications

  • Input Bulk Storage: Standard series (KZE) or enhanced ripple (KY) depending on power level
  • Output Filtering: Typically lower ESR series (KY) for high-frequency applications
  • High-Power (>5kW): Consider snap-in or screw-terminal for higher capacitance and current handling

Motor Drive Applications

  • DC Bus Applications: High ripple series (KY) or snap-in/screw-terminal for high capacitance
  • Vibration Environments: Consider mechanical mounting options carefully
  • Extended Life Requirements: Enhanced life series with appropriate derating

Automotive Applications

  • High-Temperature Environments: Use AEC-Q200 qualified series with extended temperature rating
  • Vibration Resistance: Enhanced mechanical construction recommended
  • Reliability Critical: Apply additional derating and select higher life-rated series

FAE Note: The selection process should always include simulation and testing under worst-case conditions. NCC's FAE team can provide detailed application-specific recommendations based on your circuit topology and operating conditions. For complex applications with multiple requirements, consider a mixed approach using different capacitor types in parallel.
