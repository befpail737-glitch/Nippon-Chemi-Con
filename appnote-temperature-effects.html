Temperature Effects on Capacitor Performance

Published: October 15, 2024 by LiTong FAE Team Reading time: 10 minutes

Understanding how temperature impacts Nippon Chemi-Con (NCC) capacitor parameters, performance characteristics, and operational life. This guide explores the thermal behavior of electrolytic capacitors and provides guidance for thermal management in your applications.

Understanding Temperature Impact on Capacitors

Temperature is one of the most critical factors affecting the performance and longevity of aluminum electrolytic capacitors. NCC capacitors are designed to operate reliably over a wide temperature range (typically -40°C to +105°C), but understanding the effects of temperature on various parameters is crucial for optimal application design.

Primary Temperature Effects

Temperature affects several key parameters of aluminum electrolytic capacitors:

1. Capacitance Value

Aluminum electrolytic capacitors typically exhibit a negative temperature coefficient of capacitance. At low temperatures (below 0°C), the effective capacitance may decrease by 10-20%. At high temperatures, the capacitance tends to remain stable or slightly increase.

2. Equivalent Series Resistance (ESR)

ESR shows a strong temperature dependence, decreasing significantly at higher temperatures due to increased electrolyte conductivity. However, at very high temperatures, the ESR may begin to increase due to changes in electrolyte composition.

3. Dissipation Factor (tan δ)

Similar to ESR, dissipation factor decreases with rising temperature but increases at very high temperatures when electrolyte properties deteriorate.

4. Leakage Current

Leakage current increases exponentially with temperature, following the Arrhenius relationship. Higher temperatures accelerate ionic movement, increasing leakage.

5. Operational Life

The most significant temperature effect is on operational life. As mentioned in our life calculation guide, operational life approximately doubles for every 10°C decrease in operating temperature.

Thermal Management Best Practices

1. Heat Source Separation

Position capacitors away from high-power components, switching nodes, and other significant heat sources. A minimum distance of 10mm is recommended between capacitors and heat-generating components.

2. Ventilation and Airflow

Provide adequate ventilation in enclosed systems, especially when operating near maximum temperature ratings. Consider forced cooling for high-power applications.

3. PCB Thermal Design

Use thermal vias and copper planes to dissipate heat from SMD capacitors. For high-power applications, consider using thicker copper layers (2oz or more) under capacitors.

4. Heat Sinks

For high-power applications with screw-terminal capacitors, ensure proper heat sinking to maintain case temperatures within the rated range.

Low Temperature Operation

At low temperatures:

  • ESR increases significantly, potentially causing performance issues in switching applications
  • Capacitance may decrease in some conditions
  • Start-up inrush current may increase due to higher ESR
  • Allow for warm-up periods in critical applications

For applications in cold environments, verify performance at the minimum operating temperature, especially for applications sensitive to ESR changes.

Thermal Cycling Effects

Repeated temperature cycling can cause mechanical stress on the internal construction, potentially leading to:

  • Increase in ESR over time
  • Reduction in capacitance value
  • Seal degradation accelerating electrolyte loss

NCC capacitors are designed to withstand thermal cycling, but for applications with severe cycling requirements (large ΔT or frequent cycles), consider selecting series with enhanced cycling capabilities.

Derating Guidelines

To ensure reliable operation across the full temperature range:

Voltage Derating

Consider voltage derating at high temperatures to compensate for potential changes in dielectric properties and improve long-term reliability.

Current Derating

At elevated temperatures, reduce the allowed ripple current to account for the reduced dissipation capability of the capacitor.

FAE Note: For applications with elevated ambient temperatures (>85°C), consider using NCC series with extended life ratings (8,000-10,000 hours) and implement aggressive thermal management strategies. The KY series is particularly suitable for such applications.

Testing and Validation

For critical applications, validate performance under worst-case temperature conditions:

  • Verify circuit performance at maximum and minimum operating temperatures
  • Measure actual component temperatures under full load conditions
  • Perform thermal imaging to identify hot spots
  • Validate ESR and capacitance values at operating temperatures
