Failure Analysis

Understanding common failure modes of Nippon Chemi-Con (NCC) capacitors and prevention strategies. Our comprehensive failure analysis resources help engineers identify root causes and implement preventive measures for improved system reliability.

Electrical Failures

Overvoltage, overcurrent, and ESR degradation issues.

Thermal Failures

Issues related to excessive heat and temperature cycling.

Mechanical Failures

Vibration, shock, and mounting-related failures.

Environmental Failures

Moisture, corrosion, and chemical exposure issues.

Common Failure Modes

Overtemperature Degradation

Elevated temperatures accelerate chemical reactions and cause electrolyte evaporation.

Symptoms: Increased ESR, reduced capacitance, venting, case bulging

Causes: High ambient temperature, excessive ripple current, poor thermal management

Prevention: Proper derating, adequate cooling, selecting higher temperature rated parts

Overvoltage Failure

Voltage exceeding rated limits causes dielectric breakdown and failure.

Symptoms: Short circuit, physical damage, smoke, electrolyte leakage

Causes: Surge events, reverse voltage, inadequate voltage derating

Prevention: Proper voltage derating, surge protection, correct polarity installation

Ripple Current Overstress

Excessive ripple current causes internal heating and accelerated aging.

Symptoms: Premature failure, increased ESR, shortened life

Causes: High ripple currents, high frequency operation, parallel resonance

Prevention: Calculate ripple current properly, select appropriate parts, thermal management

Dry-Out Failure

Loss of electrolyte reduces capacitance and increases ESR over time.

Symptoms: Gradual capacitance reduction, ESR increase, end-of-life failure

Causes: Normal aging process, elevated temperature, frequent thermal cycling

Prevention: Maintain operating temperature, select extended life parts, proper design

Failure Analysis Process

1

Sample Collection

Properly collect and document failed components with operational history.

2

Visual Inspection

Examine physical characteristics including case, terminals, and sealing.

3

Electrical Testing

Measure capacitance, ESR, leakage current, and insulation resistance.

4

Root Cause Analysis

Determine failure mechanism and contributing factors.

Prevention Strategies

Proper Derating

Apply appropriate voltage, temperature, and ripple current derating based on application requirements. NCC recommends at least 20% voltage derating for most applications.

Thermal Management

Implement proper heat sinking, ventilation, and layout to maintain capacitors within rated temperature limits.

Correct Installation

Ensure proper polarity, mechanical mounting, and electrical connections to prevent stress-related failures.

Environmental Protection

Protect capacitors from moisture, contamination, and corrosive environments with appropriate enclosures.

Failure Analysis Case Studies

SMPS Capacitor Premature Failure

Analysis of electrolytic capacitors failing after 12 months in switching power supply.

Root cause: Excessive ripple current due to resonant circuit interaction. Solution: Replaced with higher ripple-rated parts and circuit modification.

Automotive Application Failure

Capacitor failures in vehicle ECU units due to thermal cycling.

Root cause: Inadequate thermal relief during soldering and high temperature operation. Solution: Improved mounting and thermal management design.
