KY Series High Ripple Current Radial Lead Aluminum Electrolytic Capacitors

The KY series is engineered for applications requiring high ripple current handling with enhanced reliability. These capacitors feature superior ripple handling capabilities, extended operational life of 8,000 to 10,000 hours at +105°C, and are ideal for industrial power supplies, motor drives, and other high-demand applications.

Performance Characteristics

ESR Values

The KY series offers lower ESR values compared to standard series, enhancing performance in high-frequency applications. This contributes to reduced I²R losses and improved efficiency.

Ripple Current

Engineered with superior ripple current capabilities, allowing for higher ripple current handling in demanding applications compared to standard series.

Thermal Performance

Enhanced thermal management design enables improved heat dissipation, supporting extended operational life in high-temperature environments.

Reliability & Life

Advanced electrolyte formulation and construction techniques provide 8,000-10,000 hour life ratings at +105°C, making these capacitors ideal for applications requiring long-term reliability.

Selection Guide

When selecting capacitors from the KY series, consider these key parameters:

Ripple Current Calculation

Calculate the actual ripple current in your application. The KY series can handle significantly higher ripple currents than standard series, but it's important to stay within the specified limits for optimal life.

Thermal Management

Although these capacitors have enhanced thermal characteristics, proper thermal design remains critical. Consider factors like airflow, proximity to other heat sources, and ambient temperature when selecting case size.

Life Expectancy Estimation

Use NCC's life prediction models to estimate actual operational life under your specific operating conditions. The 8,000-10,000 hour rating is at +105°C and full rated voltage.

FAE Note: The KY series is ideal for applications with high ripple current requirements (typically 1.5x or higher than standard applications). For general purpose applications, the KZE series may be more cost-effective.
