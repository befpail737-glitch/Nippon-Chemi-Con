The KMG series features a compact design with high capacitance density, ideal for applications where board space is at a premium. These miniature radial lead capacitors maintain excellent electrical characteristics while offering space-saving benefits for high-density mounting. Perfect for portable devices, space-constrained power supplies, and applications requiring miniaturization.

Performance Characteristics

Miniaturized Design

The KMG series achieves high capacitance values in compact case sizes, making it ideal for space-constrained applications. Despite the smaller form factor, these capacitors maintain reliable electrical performance.

Capacitance Density

Optimized design maximizes capacitance per unit volume, allowing for efficient use of limited PCB space while meeting application requirements.

Thermal Considerations

Due to the compact size, thermal management becomes critical in high-power applications. Ensure adequate spacing and cooling to maximize operational life.

Mounting Flexibility

Radial lead design allows for versatile mounting configurations, including vertical mounting to reduce PCB footprint when board space is extremely limited.

Selection Guide

When selecting capacitors from the KMG series, consider these key parameters:

Capacitance Requirements

The KMG series is optimized for applications where physical space is more critical than capacitance value. Consider carefully if the space savings justify the specific characteristics of this series.

Thermal Management

Due to the compact size, heat dissipation can be challenging. Ensure adequate spacing between components and consider thermal management needs in your design. In high-power applications, standard series may be more appropriate.

Mounting Considerations

The KMG series supports both horizontal and vertical mounting options, allowing for flexible PCB layout design. Consider the impact on airflow and accessibility during manufacturing and repair.

FAE Note: The KMG series is ideal for applications where board space is at a premium and where power dissipation requirements allow for the compact form factor. For higher power applications with significant heat generation, consider conventional case sizes.
