Polymer Capacitor Market Growth Drives Innovation at NCC

Published: Aug 20, 2024 by LiTong News Team Reading time: 4 minutes

Nippon Chemi-Con (NCC) announces increased investment in conductive polymer capacitor research and development as the market for these advanced components experiences rapid growth driven by automotive electrification and consumer electronics demand.

R&D Market Trends Polymer Capacitors

Responding to Market Demand

Market analysis indicates that the global conductive polymer capacitor market is projected to grow at a compound annual growth rate of 7.8% over the next five years, driven primarily by increased adoption in automotive electronics and high-efficiency power supplies. NCC is positioned to capitalize on this trend with enhanced R&D investment and expanded production capabilities.

"The demand for polymer capacitors continues to exceed industry projections," noted Dr. Akira Sato, Chief Technology Officer at NCC. "Our enhanced R&D investment focuses on improving low-ESR performance while maintaining the reliability our customers expect."

Technological Advances

NCC's R&D efforts are concentrated on several key areas:

  • Advanced conductive polymer materials with superior conductivity
  • Enhanced cathode structure for reduced ESR
  • Improved thermal management for high-temperature applications
  • Miniaturization while maintaining capacitance values
  • Enhanced ripple current capabilities

Recent innovations have resulted in new product series that achieve 20% lower ESR values compared to previous generations while maintaining the same case sizes. These advancements are particularly valuable for applications in automotive power systems and high-efficiency DC-DC converters.

Market Applications

The growing polymer capacitor market is driven by several key applications:

Automotive Electronics

Electric vehicle power systems, ADAS, and infotainment systems require high-reliability, low-ESR capacitors.

Consumer Electronics

Smartphones, tablets, and laptops drive demand for miniaturized, high-performance capacitors.

Industrial Power

Motor drives and power supplies benefit from high ripple current handling capabilities.

Telecommunications

Base stations and networking equipment require stable performance under varying thermal conditions.

New Product Developments

NCC recently introduced two new polymer capacitor series:

  • CCAP Series: Ultra-low ESR designs for high-frequency applications in DC-DC converters
  • HCPP Series: Hybrid polymer/electrolytic technology combining polymer advantages with high capacitance values

Both series have achieved AEC-Q200 qualification for automotive applications, expanding NCC's presence in the rapidly growing EV market segment.

Production Capacity Expansion

Alongside R&D investment, NCC has announced expansion of polymer capacitor production capacity by 30% at its primary manufacturing facility. The expansion includes installation of new polymer coating equipment and automated assembly systems to meet growing demand while maintaining quality standards.

Production capacity for the popular CCAP series will increase by 40% by mid-2024, while the newer HCPP series will have dedicated production lines for specialized applications.

FAE Note: The growth in polymer capacitor demand reflects the industry shift toward high-efficiency power systems. NCC's advanced polymer series offer significant advantages in applications where low ESR and high ripple current are critical, such as in automotive and high-performance computing applications.

Future Outlook

NCC expects continued growth in the polymer capacitor segment, particularly in automotive and renewable energy applications. Future developments will focus on improving performance at elevated temperatures and developing specialized series for specific application requirements.

Customers can anticipate new product announcements throughout 2024 featuring enhanced performance specifications and application-specific optimizations that leverage NCC's expertise in conductive polymer technology.
